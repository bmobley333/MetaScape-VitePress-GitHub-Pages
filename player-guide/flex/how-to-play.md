# How to Play (Flex Engine)

The **MetaScape Flex Engine** is a narrative-driven, rules-light TTRPG system. It prioritizes fast pacing, creative player actions, and cinematic storytelling, backed by a simple and fluid attribute system.

---

## 🎬 Core Philosophy & Guiding Principles

* **Cinematic**: Every roll carries weight. Tremendous successes and Critical setbacks create stories worth retelling.
* **Fast**: Rules are built for speed. When in doubt, simplify to keep the narrative moving.
* **Open**: Any character concept works with GM approval.
* **Inclusive**: Any character can attempt any skill, weapon, or armor.
* **Tactical**: Avoidance is divided into **Dodge** (Motion) and **Block** (Might), forcing moment-to-moment choices.
* **GM-Light**: The GM sets difficulties and modifiers; players roll all the dice (except the GM's quick *Intensity Die*).

---

## 🎲 Core Die Mechanics

All ability checks in the Flex engine follow a simple unified formula:

$$\text{Roll} = \text{\#d20} + \text{Attribute Rating} + \text{Modifiers vs. Difficulty}$$

* **Attribute Rating**: Roll your attribute die (which matches your attribute's numeric rating: **4, 6, 8, 10, or 12**). These represent die sizes (d4, d6, d8, d10, or d12), but are displayed as numbers only.
* **Modifiers**: Add modifiers from gear, traits, or powers (Max +5 combined).

### Roll Combinations (#d20)

Depending on your skill level and situational advantages, you roll different numbers of d20s:

| Roll Type | Dice Rolled | How to Resolve |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **Unskilled with Disadvantage** | `2L20` | Roll two d20, keep the lowest. |
| **Skilled with Disadvantage** or **Unskilled** | `1d20` | Roll one d20. |
| **Skilled** or **Unskilled with Advantage** | `2H20` | Roll two d20, keep the highest. |
| **Skilled with Advantage** | `3H20` | Roll three d20, keep the highest. |

---

## ✅ The Five Attributes

Characters are defined by five core attributes:

1. **Might💪**: governs brute strength, melee attacks/damage, blocking defense, and armor sizes.
2. **Motion🏃**: governs initiative (Nish), dodging, athletics, and hurled weapons.
3. **Mind👁️**: governs shot weapons, intellect, awareness, and social checks.
4. **Magic✨**: governs supernatural abilities, saves, resistances, and using magic items.
5. **Moxie🫀**: governs stamina, physical resistances, and base Vitality (Vit❤️).

---

## 🧾 Character Creation & Attribute Assignment

During character creation, you assign starting **Tiers** (**S, A, B, C, D**) across the five attributes. 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Priority Matters**: When assigning your attribute tiers, the order in which you place them matters even for numerical ties. Place attributes in highest to lowest priority.

## ⚔️ Combat Flow

A round in combat is governed by the **AMP** action system: Characters have an Attack (⚔️A), Move (👣M), and Partial (✋P) action, plus Free reactions.

### Player Attacking (Action ⚔️A)
1. **Roll to Hit**: Roll your attack check (`2H20` if skilled, `1d20` if unskilled) + governing Attribute Rating + Modifiers vs. Monster Defense.
2. **Roll Damage**: On a hit, roll your weapon's damage rating (4–12) and subtract the Monster's Armor Rating (AR). The remainder reduces the monster's Vitality.
   * *Damage Uplifting*: If using single-handed, two-handed, weapon & shield, or martial arts styles, your weapon damage rating scales up to match your governing attribute rating (Might💪 for Melee, Motion🏃 for Hurled, Mind👁️ for Shot).
   * *Natural Weapon Damage*: Natural weapons (like Brawl🥊 or Throw Object) do not use weapon tables. Their damage rating is automatically **one step below** the governing attribute rating (in the sequence 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, minimum 4).

### Player Defending (Monster Attacking)
1. **Avoid Attack**: Choose to **Dodge🏃 (Motion)** or **Block🧱 (Might)**. Roll `2H20` + Attribute Rating vs. Monster Attack value.
2. **Armor Absorption (AR🧥)**: If avoid fails, roll your Armor Rating (AR) and subtract the result from the Monster's Damage. Any remaining damage becomes wounds.
3. **Fatigue**: Even if your armor fully absorbs the damage, you suffer a minimum of 1 wound (representing combat fatigue) whenever you are hit.

## 🌟 Tremendous & 💀 Critical
* **Tremendous🌟**: A natural 20 on a kept d20. Triggers beneficial narrative twists.
* **Critical💀**: A natural 1 on a kept d20. Triggers complications or setbacks.
